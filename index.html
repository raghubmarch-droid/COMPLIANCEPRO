Common Questions

DPDP Act FAQs

Everything Indian businesses need to know about the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023.

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 is India's landmark data privacy legislation enacted to protect personal data of Indian citizens. It governs how businesses must collect, process, store, and delete personal data, and grants data principals (individuals) several rights over their data. Non-compliance attracts penalties up to ₹250 Crore.

Any entity — startup, SME, or enterprise — that operates in India and collects, processes, or stores personal data of Indian citizens must comply with the DPDP Act 2023. This includes e-commerce platforms, fintech companies, healthcare providers, HR software, and virtually any digital business with Indian users.

A DPDP gap analysis compares your current data practices against DPDP Act requirements to identify compliance shortfalls. It prioritises remediation actions and gives your legal, tech, and leadership teams a clear roadmap. Our AI-powered gap analysis tool delivers this assessment in minutes — free.

The DPDP Act 2023 prescribes penalties up to ₹250 Crore for data breaches and up to ₹200 Crore for failure to implement reasonable security safeguards. Repeated violations can attract higher cumulative penalties. The Data Protection Board of India will adjudicate complaints.

For most organisations, a foundational DPDP compliance programme takes 8–16 weeks depending on complexity. With our AI-native platform, the gap analysis is instant, privacy notices can be generated in days, and training can be deployed organisation-wide within a week. We also offer accelerated startup fast-tracks.

Significant Data Fiduciaries under the DPDP Act must appoint a Data Protection Officer based in India. The DPO serves as the point of contact for data principals and the Data Protection Board. Our DPO-as-a-Service offering provides experienced DPOs on a retainer basis for organisations not ready for a full-time hire.
